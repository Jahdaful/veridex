# VERIDEX FORENSIC AI — ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

**Effective Date:** 2026-05-24
**Version:** 1.0
**Classification:** Law Enforcement Use Only

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines the standards of conduct required of all Authorized Users of the VERIDEX Forensic AI System. This policy supplements the Terms of Service and must be read in conjunction with it.

---

## 2. AUTHORIZED USERS

Access is restricted to:

| User Type | Conditions |
|---|---|
| Law Enforcement Officers | Active duty, with case-related authorization |
| Licensed Forensic Investigators | Valid professional license, engaged on authorized matter |
| Certified Digital Forensics Professionals | Relevant certification (EnCE, GCFE, GCFA, CCE, or equivalent) |
| Agency IT/Security Staff | System administration purposes only |
| Authorized Trainees | Under direct supervision of qualified personnel |

---

## 3. PERMITTED ACTIVITIES

✓ Analyzing digital media in connection with an active, lawfully authorized investigation  
✓ Generating investigative reports for inclusion in official case files  
✓ Supporting the work of certified forensic examiners as a preliminary screening tool  
✓ Professional training under Agency supervision using approved training materials  
✓ System testing using synthetic or non-sensitive media authorized by the Agency  

---

## 4. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

### 4.1 Unauthorized Subjects
✗ Analyzing media relating to individuals who are not subjects of an authorized investigation  
✗ Using the System to investigate colleagues, superiors, or personal acquaintances  
✗ Processing media obtained through illegal means or in violation of applicable search and seizure laws  

### 4.2 Misuse of Results
✗ Presenting AI-generated analysis as conclusive expert opinion without independent verification  
✗ Altering, falsifying, or selectively presenting System outputs to mislead any person or body  
✗ Using results to justify actions that would otherwise require judicial authorization  
✗ Sharing reports with unauthorized parties including members of the public, media, or opposing counsel without Agency authorization  

### 4.3 System Integrity
✗ Attempting to bypass, probe, or attack any security feature of the System  
✗ Sharing access credentials with any other person  
✗ Uploading malicious files, excessively large files, or files designed to crash or exploit the System  
✗ Reverse engineering or attempting to extract the underlying AI model or system architecture  

### 4.4 Privacy Violations
✗ Processing intimate images, images of minors, or other sensitive material outside of an authorized investigation  
✗ Using the System to build profiles of individuals for purposes unrelated to authorized investigations  
✗ Retaining or distributing GPS metadata or other location data extracted from analyzed files outside of proper evidence handling procedures  

---

## 5. HANDLING OF SENSITIVE MATERIAL

When submitting sensitive media (e.g., CSAM investigations, victim imagery, classified material):
- Ensure proper Agency authorization is in place before submission
- Note that file content may be transmitted to a third-party AI API (Anthropic). Review Section 5 of the Privacy Policy before submitting sensitive material.
- Maintain strict chain-of-custody documentation
- Follow Agency-specific protocols for sensitive evidence handling

---

## 6. REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

Authorized Users must report:
- Any suspected unauthorized access to the System
- Any System behavior that appears incorrect or potentially compromised
- Any inadvertent disclosure of System access credentials
- Any output that appears clearly erroneous or inconsistent with known facts

Reports should be directed to the System Administrator and the Agency's information security officer.

---

## 7. CONSEQUENCES OF MISUSE

Violations of this AUP may result in:
- Immediate revocation of System access
- Disciplinary proceedings under applicable Agency policy
- Civil or criminal liability
- Referral to professional licensing bodies
- Evidence suppression in affected proceedings

---

## 8. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

Use of this System constitutes acknowledgement that you have read, understood, and will comply with this Acceptable Use Policy.
